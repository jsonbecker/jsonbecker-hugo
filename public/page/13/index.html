A little knowledge is a wonderful (dangerous) thing

November 20, 2012
∞
It is so tempting to try to apply cognitive science results in education. It seems like an obvious step on the long road of moving education from a field of theory and philosophies to one more grounded in empirical research. Yet, learning myths are persistent. Even scarier, “those who know the most about neuroscience also believe the most myths.“ Educators may have the best intentions when trying to infuse their practice with evidence, but they all too often are not equipped as critical consumers of research.
